Resources Criteria for AOT

Does the individual meet the criteria for AOT? [[Yes.|petition_initial]] OR [[No.|aot_inappropriate]]

Generally, an adult with a mental illness is a candidate for assisted outpatient treatment if they meet one or more of the following criteria. The immediacy of the risk of harm will determine whether hospitalization or AOT is the appropriate course of treatment.

  1. The individual can be expected in the near future to seriously physically injure themselves or another person and has previously engaged in acts or made threats that support that expectation.
  2. The individual who has mental illness, and as a result of that mental illness is unable to attend to those of his or her basic physical needs such as food, clothing, or shelter that must be attended to in order for the individual to avoid serious harm in the near future, and who has demonstrated that inability by failing to attend to those basic physical needs.
  3. The individual’s judgment is impaired by mental illness, lacks an understanding of the need for treatment and demonstrates an unwillingness to voluntarily participate in or adhere to treatment that is necessary to prevent a relapse or harmful deterioration thereby presenting a substantial risk of significant physical or mental harm to the individual or others.

If possible, obtain a voluntary assessment for AOT. :: deferral {"position":"700,300","size":"100,100"} /*THIS IS THE SECTION HEADER*/ /*THIS IS THE LOWER BOX*/

[[The individual chooses to defer.|deferral_adherence]] OR [[The individual does not defer.|stipulate_initial]]

The individual has the right to request to temporarily defer the hearing. Deferrals can be up to 60 days of hospitalization and 180 days of outpatient treatment. During this timeframe, the following is true:

  • The individual may demand a hearing.
  • The individual must agree to accept the proposed treatment.
  • The original petition and clinical certificates will remain valid.
  • If the individual refuses proposed treatment at any time, a hearing can be held.

If the individual is hospitalized, a deferral conference will be held with hospital staff, CMH staff, and the individual’s defense attorney. If hospitalization is not requested in the petition, the individual should still be given the offer to defer.

  • End of deferral into initial order
    • No less than 7 or more than 14 days before a deferral expires, a Demand for Hearing can be submitted to request an initial court order if it is believed the individual will discontinue services at the end of the deferral period.

Non-adherence should be addressed by the provider to try to engage the individual in following their treatment order. If engagement attempts are unsuccessful or the level of risk is high , the CMH may submit a notification of noncompliance and request any of the following:

  • assessment of the individual
